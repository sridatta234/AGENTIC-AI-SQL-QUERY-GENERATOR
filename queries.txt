### Easy Questions

1.  List the first 10 films in the database, ordered alphabetically by title.
2.  Find the total number of actors in the `actor` table.

---

### Medium Questions

1.  Find the name of each film category and the number of films within each category.
2.  Calculate the total revenue generated by each store (Hint: You'll need to join the `store`, `staff`, and `payment` tables).
3.  List the top 5 most rented films. The result should show the film title and the rental count, ordered from most rented to least.
4.  Find all customers who have rented at least 25 films. Display their first name, last name, and the total number of rentals.
5.  Show the average rental duration (in days) for films grouped by their rating (e.g., 'G', 'PG', 'NC-17').
6.  List the first and last names of all actors who have appeared in a film titled 'ACADEMY DINOSAUR'.
7.  Find the number of films each actor has appeared in, but only show actors who have been in more than 40 films.

---

### Hard Questions

1.  Find the film category that has the highest average rental rate. You should return only the category name and its average rental rate.
2.  For each store, find the top 3 customers who have spent the most money on rentals. The result should include the store ID, customer's full name, and their total payment amount. (Hint: Use a window function like `RANK()` or `DENSE_RANK()`).
3.  Write a query to get a monthly and yearly count of rentals. The result should have three columns: `rental_year`, `rental_month`, and `rental_count`.
4.  Find all films that have never been rented. (Hint: This can be solved with a subquery or a `LEFT JOIN`).








Consecutive Rentals: Identify customers who have rented films on three consecutive days. List the customer's full name and the dates of the three rentals.

Top Genres Per Customer: For each customer, find the film category they have rented from most frequently.

Film Rental Rate Fluctuations: Find the average rental rate for each film category, but only include rentals that occurred on weekends (Saturday and Sunday).

Longest Unrented Film: Find the film that has gone the longest without being rented. Return the film title and the number of days since its last rental.

Most Valuable Customers Per Country: For each country, list the top 5 customers who have generated the most total revenue. The result should include the country name, customer's name, and total revenue.

Staff Rental Discrepancies: Find the staff member with the largest difference between their highest and lowest single payment amount.

Rental-to-Return Ratio: Calculate the percentage of films returned late by each store.

Customer Rental Trends: For each month of 2005, calculate the number of unique customers who made a rental.

Store Revenue by Category: Show the total revenue for each film category, broken down by store. The result should have columns for store_id, category_name, and total_revenue.

The "Film-Rental-Free" Day: Find the date with the highest number of consecutive film releases without any rentals. Return the start date and the duration in days.
